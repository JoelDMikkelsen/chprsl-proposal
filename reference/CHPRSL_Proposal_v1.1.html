The solution stack

NetSuite handles consolidation; three vendors handle the rest

NetSuite is the accounting and analytics consolidation layer. Zudello delivers procure-to-pay. NetGain delivers fixed assets. Revolut delivers corporate cards and staff expenses. Each vendor supplies its own integration and is licensed separately; the configurator below shows every line.

◎

NetSuite ERP

SuiteSuccess Financials First Standard. Multi-entity consolidation, GL, AP/AR, banking, fixed-asset ledger, reporting. Native AI (MCP, exception management, NetSuite Expert) included at no extra cost.

↔

Zudello

End-to-end procure-to-pay: supplier onboarding, POs, goods receipting, 3-way matching, AP capture, DOA approvals, budget-vs-spend. No per-user charges. Vendor-supplied NetSuite integration.

◈

NetAsset

NetGain SuiteApp for fixed-asset management. Runs inside NetSuite, so no integration build is required. Replaces native NetSuite Fixed Assets; sized to the ~3,000-asset register consolidated across all group entities.

◢

Revolut

Corporate card + expense management. Vendor-supplied API integration into NetSuite. Retains the UX your team already likes; removes manual corporate-card tracking entirely.

▶

Fusion5 CSV Integrator

Scheduled flat-file ingestion for SwiftPOS, Concilio, Micropay summary journals. Eliminates manual journal entry; provides a consistent, auditable data path into the ERP.

✉

Fusion5 Bank Parser

ABA file generation from approved NetSuite payment runs. The outbound payment cycle is auditable end-to-end within NetSuite. Advanced Electronic Bank Payments handles the inbound bank feeds.

Phase 1 scope

Phase 1 delivers consolidation; Phase 2 items remain visible in the roadmap

Phase 1 is sized to hit 1 July 2026. Items that are not required for consolidation and close are deferred to Phase 2 and listed alongside the in-scope items below.

In scope for Phase 1

  • General Ledger across 12 entities + consolidated reporting
  • Multi-entity subsidiary management & intercompany netting
  • Accounts Payable (bills flow from Zudello into NetSuite)
  • Accounts Receivable & cash application
  • Advanced Electronic Bank Payments (inbound + outbound ABA)
  • Fixed Assets via NetAsset: ~3,000 assets, single depreciation schedule, low-value pool
  • Procurement end-to-end in Zudello (no NetSuite GA licences for receipters)
  • Corporate card & expense via Revolut integration
  • Job costing via native custom segment (capital, events, marketing)
  • Contract Management: custom record plus SuiteFlow approvals
  • Vendor Bank Details Approval Workflow
  • Journal Approval Workflow (manual journals)
  • SuiteAnalytics BI dashboards & Financial Report Builder
  • Integrations: SwiftPOS, Concilio, Micropay (via CSV Integrator)
  • Data migration: opening balances + master data
  • Training (End-User Essentials + Admin Essentials)

Deferred to Phase 2

  • NetSuite EPM: Planning & Budgeting
  • Advanced Revenue Management (spreadsheets continue in Phase 1)
  • NetSuite Analytics Warehouse
  • Middleware / message bus for Phase 2 architecture
  • Field Service Management & CMMS (separate evaluation in flight)
  • Inventory management in NetSuite (remains in SwiftPOS)
  • HR & Payroll in NetSuite (decision pending)
  • CRM / marketing automation
  • Custom SuiteScript development
  • Gaming-specific direct integrations (flow via Concilio)
  • Post-live managed services (separate PartnerPlus engagement)

Commercial summary

The Fusion5 implementation is fixed at $165,000; vendor licensing runs direct with Zudello, NetGain and Revolut and is modelled line-by-line in the configurator.

Zudello's licensing model is transaction-based, so the users who approve payments, raise POs or receipt goods access the platform without adding to the cost line. NetAsset is priced per asset, with the tier sized to the consolidated register across all group entities. Variations on the Fusion5 implementation are managed as formal change requests.
